Trompe-l'oeil (French for 'deceive the eye') is an art term for the highly realistic optical illusion of three-dimensional space and objects on a two-dimensional surface. Trompe-l'oeil tricks the viewer into perceiving painted objects or spaces as real.

Trompe-l'oeil is usually associated with still life paintings but this beautiful landscape painting by Thomas Creswick is another great example of this realistic approach. It looks like the rock in the foreground is about to fall out of the frame!
